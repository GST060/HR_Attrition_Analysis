📊 HR Attrition & Employee Performance Analysis

A Data Analytics project focused on identifying the key drivers of employee attrition and performance trends using HR data. The goal is to provide data-driven insights and actionable recommendations that help organizations reduce employee turnover and improve workforce management.


📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is a critical issue that affects productivity, hiring costs, and organizational stability. This project analyzes HR employee data to understand:
•	Why employees leave the company
•	Which departments experience the highest attrition
•	How salary, overtime, and work-life balance impact employee retention
•	What factors influence employee performance
Through data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, and SQL-based business analysis, this project identifies patterns in employee behavior and provides recommendations for HR decision-making.


🗂 Dataset Information

The dataset contains 1,473 employee records across multiple departments with various attributes related to employee demographics, job roles, and performance.
Key Features
Category	Attributes
Employee Info	Age, Gender, Distance from Home
Job Details	Department, Job Role, Years at Company
Compensation	Monthly Income, Salary Band
Work Environment	Work-Life Balance, Overtime
Satisfaction	Job Satisfaction, Relationship Satisfaction
Performance	Performance Rating, Training Times
Attrition	Attrition Status (Yes/No)

📈 Project Workflow

1️.  Data Loading
The HR dataset was imported into Excel and SQL for analysis.


2️.  Data Cleaning (Excel)

Performed essential preprocessing steps:
•	Removed duplicate records
•	Checked for missing values
•	Standardized data formats
•	Created calculated columns such as:
o	Age Group
o	Salary Band
o	Attrition Flags
This step ensured the dataset was accurate and analysis-ready.


3️.  Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Using Excel Pivot Tables, multiple analyses were performed:
•	Attrition by Department
•	Salary vs Attrition
•	Overtime vs Attrition
•	Job Satisfaction Distribution
•	Work-Life Balance Analysis
•	Employee Tenure Analysis
•	Department Performance Trends
•	Gender-based Attrition Analysis
These analyses helped uncover initial patterns and trends in the dataset.


4️.  SQL Business Analysis

SQL queries were written to answer real HR business questions, such as:
•	Which departments have the highest attrition rates?
•	What employee profiles are most likely to leave?
•	Does overtime significantly increase attrition risk?
•	How does salary impact employee retention?
•	Which job roles have the highest turnover?
The queries included:
•	Aggregations
•	Filtering
•	Grouping
•	Attrition rate calculations


📊 Key Insights

The analysis revealed several critical findings:
🔹 Overall Attrition
•	16.1% employee attrition rate
•	Approximately 1 in 6 employees leaves the company
🔹 Department Risk
•	Sales department has the highest attrition rate
•	R&D has lower attrition but larger employee volume
🔹 Compensation Impact
•	Employees earning below ₹5,000/month account for nearly 69% of attrition
🔹 Overtime Risk
•	Employees working overtime leave at almost 3× higher rate
🔹 Early Tenure Attrition
•	Employees in their first 2 years show the highest turnover risk
🔹 Work-Life Balance
•	Employees with poor work-life balance have significantly higher attrition
